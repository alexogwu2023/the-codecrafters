In Go, operators are symbols that perform operations on values or variables. 
**Arithmetic operators** like `+`, `-`, `*`, `/`, and `%` are used for math. 
**assignment operators** like `=` and `+=` store values in variables. 
**Comparison operators** such as `==`, `!=`, `<`, `>`, `<=`, `>=` check relationships between values and return `true` or `false`.

Go also has 
**logical operators** (`&&` for AND, `||` for OR, `!` for NOT) to combine or invert conditions. **bitwise operators** (`&`, `|`, `^`, `<<`, `>>`) to manipulate numbers at the binary level. These operators let you perform calculations, make decisions, and control how your program behaves.
